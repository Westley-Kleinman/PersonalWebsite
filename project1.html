Carbon Rim Impact Testing Machine

UCI-Compliant Testing Equipment for Professional Cycling

Designed and manufactured a precision impact testing machine for Boyd Cycling during my Summer 2024 internship. This UCI-compliant equipment enables rigorous testing of carbon fiber bicycle rims with controlled 200J+ impact forces.

Problem Identification

The Engineering Challenge

Industry Testing Gap

Boyd Cycling required a precision impact testing machine to validate carbon rim durability according to UCI standards. Commercial testing equipment was prohibitively expensive (often $50,000+), lacked customization for specific rim geometries, and couldn't achieve the precise 200+ joule impact forces needed for comprehensive validation testing.

Technical Requirements Analysis

The solution needed to deliver controlled, repeatable impact forces while meeting strict UCI compliance standards. Key challenges included achieving electromagnetic precision release, implementing comprehensive safety systems, integrating data collection capabilities, and designing for cost-effective manufacturing within Boyd Cycling's operational budget.

Design Constraints

  • Force Precision: ±2% accuracy for 200+ joule impacts
  • UCI Compliance: Meet International Cycling Union testing protocols
  • Safety Standards: Industrial-grade operator protection systems
  • Repeatability: Consistent results across multiple test cycles
  • Cost Target: Under $15,000 total production cost
  • Timeline: Complete design and build within 12-week internship

Design & Engineering

How I Built the Testing Machine

01

UCI Standards Analysis & Force Calculations

I began by thoroughly analyzing UCI testing protocols and conducting detailed force calculations to determine exact impact energy requirements. Using physics principles, I calculated drop heights, impact velocities, and mass requirements to achieve 200+ joule impacts. This analysis informed all subsequent design decisions and established the mathematical foundation for the entire system.

Physics Calculations UCI Protocol Analysis Force Requirements
02

Structural Frame Design & SolidWorks Modeling

Designed the main structural framework in SolidWorks, focusing on rigid vertical guides that could withstand repeated high-energy impacts. I performed finite element analysis on critical load-bearing components, optimized material selection for cost and strength, and ensured the frame could maintain precision alignment over thousands of test cycles.

SolidWorks CAD FEA Analysis Structural Engineering
03

Electromagnetic Release System Engineering

Developed a precision electromagnetic release mechanism to eliminate human timing variability. I designed the electromagnet specifications, calculated holding forces, selected appropriate power supplies, and created the control circuitry. This system ensures consistent release timing and enables repeatable test conditions across all testing scenarios.

Electromagnetic Design Control Circuitry Precision Timing
04

Manufacturing Coordination & Assembly

Coordinated with Boyd Cycling's manufacturing partners to produce precision components according to my SolidWorks specifications. I created detailed assembly drawings, established quality control checkpoints, and personally oversaw the assembly process to ensure all components met tolerance requirements and safety standards.

Manufacturing Drawings Quality Control Assembly Management
05

Safety System Integration & Testing

Implemented comprehensive safety features including emergency stops, protective barriers, operator lockout systems, and fail-safe mechanisms. I conducted extensive safety testing, validated all protection systems, created operating procedures, and ensured compliance with industrial safety standards for high-energy equipment operation.

Safety Engineering System Validation Operating Procedures
06

Calibration & UCI Compliance Validation

Performed detailed calibration procedures to ensure accurate force delivery and measurement. I conducted extensive testing against UCI standards, documented all results, implemented data logging systems, and validated that the machine consistently met or exceeded all required testing protocols for professional cycling equipment.
