Carbon Fiber Manufacturing Journey

Boyd Cycling & Time Bicycles Engineering Internship

Exploring and optimizing the carbon fiber rim manufacturing process through hands-on engineering work, focusing on the Resin Transfer Molding (RTM) process and quality improvements.

Step 01

BobinWinder Operation

The foundation of carbon fiber rim manufacturing

The BobinWinder machine is where the carbon fiber rim manufacturing process begins. This machine takes the wax mold of the rim and wraps it with two layers of carbon fiber weave, creating the structural foundation for the final product.

I worked on optimizing this machine's operation to ensure consistent fiber placement and proper layer alignment. The precision achieved at this stage directly impacts the quality and performance characteristics of the finished rim.

Step 02

Mold Placement

Positioning for the injection process

After the carbon fiber layers are applied by the BobinWinder, the wrapped rim is positioned in the injection mold. This step requires careful alignment to ensure proper resin flow patterns during the subsequent injection process.

The positioning and mold preparation directly influence how effectively the resin will saturate the carbon fiber matrix during the RTM process.

Step 03

Resin Transfer Molding (RTM)

The injection and curing process

The RTM process involves sealing the mold, applying vacuum to remove air, and injecting resin under pressure. This transforms the dry carbon fiber layers into a solid composite structure.

During my work, I observed and analyzed issues with resin distribution that led to inconsistent quality in the final products, specifically resin-poor and resin-rich zones that affected performance.

Challenge

Resin Distribution Issues

Identifying manufacturing inconsistencies

Resin-Poor Zones

Areas where insufficient resin penetration created dry spots in the carbon fiber matrix. These zones represent potential failure points and inconsistent structural properties.

Resin-Rich Zones

Areas with excess resin accumulation that add unnecessary weight without structural benefit. These zones indicate inefficient resin flow and distribution patterns.

Solutions

Process Improvement Approaches

Engineering solutions to address resin distribution challenges

Secondary Injection Points

Explored adding secondary injection and vacuum points to create multiple resin flow paths and improve saturation of complex rim geometries.

Enhanced Resin Channels

Investigated increasing the size of resin impregnation channels to improve flow volume and reduce pressure restrictions across the fiber matrix.

Temperature Optimization

Experimented with adjusting mold and resin temperatures to optimize viscosity and improve flow characteristics during the injection process.

Process Parameter Tuning

Worked on optimizing injection timing, pressure settings, and vacuum application to improve overall resin distribution and reduce defects.

Experience

Engineering Learning & Development

Key insights gained through hands-on manufacturing engineering

This internship provided valuable hands-on experience with advanced composite manufacturing processes. Working with the RTM process, I gained insights into the complexities of resin flow dynamics, the importance of process control, and the engineering challenges involved in achieving consistent quality in composite manufacturing.

The experience reinforced the importance of systematic problem identification, collaborative engineering approaches, and the iterative nature of process optimization in manufacturing environments.
