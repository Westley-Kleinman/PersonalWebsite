Demonstration of the automated bucket washing system prototype in action

Problem & Solution

Addressing CompostNow's container cleaning inefficiencies

The Challenge

CompostNow required an efficient cleaning system for their compost collection containers. Manual cleaning was time-intensive, labor-heavy, and inconsistent, creating operational bottlenecks that limited their service capacity and increased labor costs.

Design Approach

Developed an automated mechanical cleaning system focusing on reducing manual labor and improving consistency. The solution integrated rotating mechanisms, water distribution systems, and automated positioning to streamline the entire cleaning process.

Measurable Impact

Successfully reduced cleaning time by 43% through innovative mechanical design solutions. The prototype demonstrated significant efficiency improvements while maintaining cleaning quality standards required for CompostNow's operations.

Technical Development

SolidWorks design and laser cutting prototype fabrication

Design Process

Utilized SolidWorks for comprehensive 3D modeling and system design. Developed detailed mechanical assemblies incorporating rotating cleaning mechanisms, water distribution systems, and automated positioning components for optimal cleaning efficiency.

Prototype Fabrication

Applied laser cutting technologies for precision component manufacturing. Integrated multiple subsystems including mechanical drive systems, water management, and structural framework to create a functional prototype for testing and validation.

System Integration

Successfully integrated mechanical, hydraulic, and control systems into a cohesive cleaning solution. Demonstrated effective team collaboration in problem-solving, design iteration, and prototype development throughout the engineering process.
