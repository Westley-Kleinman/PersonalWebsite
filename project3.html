Design Challenge

Problem Identification

Problem Definition & User Research

Our team conducted systematic observation and user interviews to identify inefficiencies in manual bucket cleaning processes. We analyzed time-motion studies, measured cleaning effectiveness variations, and documented user pain points including physical strain, inconsistent results, and hard-to-reach surface areas that led to incomplete cleaning.

Technical Requirements & Constraints

Established quantifiable design criteria including cleaning coverage targets (95%+ surface area), cycle time goals (under 3 minutes), water efficiency parameters, and automation requirements. Defined technical constraints including power limitations, material availability, budget restrictions ($200 prototype limit), and manufacturability considerations.

Solution Architecture Planning

Developed system architecture combining mechanical rotation, pressurized water delivery, and automated bucket positioning. Created detailed component specifications, power transmission calculations, water flow requirements, and control system design to ensure all subsystems would integrate effectively in the final prototype.

Engineering Solution

Automated Washing System

Step 1: Mechanical System Design & Assembly

I designed and built the rotating brush mechanism using a DC motor with gear reduction to achieve optimal rotational speed for cleaning effectiveness. Fabricated mounting brackets to secure multiple brush assemblies at different angles, ensuring complete bucket surface coverage. Engineered the bucket positioning system with adjustable clamps to accommodate various bucket sizes and maintain stable operation during cleaning cycles.

Step 2: Water Delivery System Integration

Constructed a pressurized water delivery system using a submersible pump, pressure regulation valves, and strategically positioned spray nozzles. Calculated flow rates and pressure requirements to ensure adequate cleaning power while optimizing water usage efficiency. Integrated water filtration and recycling components to minimize waste and enable continuous operation during extended testing periods.

Step 3: Control System Programming & Testing

Developed the automation control system using Arduino microcontroller programming to coordinate brush rotation speeds, water spray timing, and cycle duration. Implemented safety interlocks, created user interface controls, and programmed automated sequences for consistent operation. Conducted iterative testing to optimize timing parameters and validate system reliability across multiple cleaning cycles.

Testing & Validation

Systematic Testing Protocol

Testing Plan Development

Developed comprehensive testing protocol to validate system performance against established design requirements. Created systematic methodology for evaluating cleaning effectiveness, operational reliability, user interface design, and overall system performance under various operating conditions and bucket configurations.

Performance Metrics & Criteria

Established quantifiable success criteria including cleaning coverage percentage, cycle time efficiency, water consumption optimization, and user satisfaction ratings. Developed standardized testing procedures for consistent measurement and evaluation across different test scenarios and team members.

Data Collection & Analysis

Implemented systematic data collection methods including before/after cleaning assessments, timing measurements, resource consumption tracking, and user feedback surveys. Applied statistical analysis techniques to validate results and identify areas for design improvement and optimization.
