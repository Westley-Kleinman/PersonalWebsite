Design Challenge

Problem Identification

Design Thinking Process

EGR101 introduced systematic engineering design methodology focusing on user-centered problem solving. Our team applied design thinking principles to identify a real-world cleaning challenge and develop a comprehensive automated solution through iterative design, prototyping, and rigorous testing protocols.

Problem Discovery & Analysis

Through structured user interviews and observational research, we identified significant inefficiencies in manual bucket cleaning across agricultural, industrial, and residential applications. Our analysis revealed consistent issues with cleaning thoroughness, time consumption, and physical strain on users.

Requirements & Constraints

Established comprehensive design requirements including automated operation cycles, multi-size bucket compatibility, water efficiency optimization, and cost-effective manufacturing potential. Developed systematic testing protocols to validate performance against quantifiable metrics and user satisfaction criteria.

Engineering Solution

Automated Washing System

Systematic Design Development

Applied structured concept generation techniques to develop multiple solution approaches. Evaluated designs using weighted decision matrices considering cleaning effectiveness, mechanical complexity, cost factors, and user accessibility. Selected integrated approach featuring coordinated brush rotation, pressurized spray system, and automated positioning.

Prototype Implementation

Constructed functional prototype incorporating mechanical drive systems, water delivery mechanisms, and control interfaces. Integrated multiple subsystems including motor-driven brush assemblies, spray nozzle arrays, and bucket securing mechanisms. Focused on demonstrating core functionality while maintaining modularity for testing and iteration.

Comprehensive Testing Protocol

Developed and executed systematic testing plan evaluating cleaning effectiveness, operational reliability, and user interface design. Conducted quantitative performance assessments including cleaning coverage analysis, cycle time optimization, and water usage efficiency measurements. Documented results against established success criteria.

Testing & Validation

Systematic Testing Protocol

Testing Plan Development

Developed comprehensive testing protocol to validate system performance against established design requirements. Created systematic methodology for evaluating cleaning effectiveness, operational reliability, user interface design, and overall system performance under various operating conditions and bucket configurations.

Performance Metrics & Criteria

Established quantifiable success criteria including cleaning coverage percentage, cycle time efficiency, water consumption optimization, and user satisfaction ratings. Developed standardized testing procedures for consistent measurement and evaluation across different test scenarios and team members.

Data Collection & Analysis

Implemented systematic data collection methods including before/after cleaning assessments, timing measurements, resource consumption tracking, and user feedback surveys. Applied statistical analysis techniques to validate results and identify areas for design improvement and optimization.
