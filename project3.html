Automated Bucket Washing System

Duke EGR101 Design Thinking Project

Design Challenge

Problem Identification

Design Thinking Process

EGR101 emphasized systematic problem identification and solution development. Our team was tasked with identifying a real-world problem that could be addressed through engineering design, then developing and prototyping a practical solution.

Problem Discovery

Through observation and user research, we identified that manual bucket cleaning in various settings (agricultural, industrial, residential) was inefficient, inconsistent, and often resulted in inadequate cleaning due to hard-to-reach areas and user fatigue.

User Requirements

Our research revealed key user needs: automated operation to reduce manual labor, consistent cleaning results, ability to handle various bucket sizes, and cost-effective implementation for small-scale operations.

Engineering Solution

Automated Washing System

Concept Development

Developed multiple design concepts and evaluated them against user requirements and engineering constraints. Selected optimal approach featuring rotating brush mechanism, water spray system, and automated bucket positioning for comprehensive cleaning coverage.

Prototype Construction

Built working prototype using available materials and fabrication methods. Integrated mechanical systems for brush rotation, water delivery, and bucket handling. Focused on demonstrating core functionality and validating design concept.

Testing & Validation

Conducted systematic testing to evaluate cleaning effectiveness, reliability, and user operation. Documented performance metrics and identified areas for improvement. Demonstrated successful proof-of-concept operation.
