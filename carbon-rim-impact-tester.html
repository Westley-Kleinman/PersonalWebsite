Carbon Rim Impact Testing

Advanced impact testing methodology for carbon fiber bicycle rims at Boyd Cycling

Materials Testing Engineering Analysis Quality Control
Boyd Cycling
Engineering Intern

Project Overview

During my internship at Boyd Cycling, I developed and implemented comprehensive impact testing protocols for carbon fiber bicycle rims. This project involved creating standardized testing procedures, analyzing failure modes, and establishing quality control benchmarks for production rims.

Key Achievements

  • 1 Designed and constructed impact testing apparatus capable of simulating real-world riding conditions
  • 2 Developed standardized testing protocols and data collection procedures
  • 3 Analyzed failure modes and established quality control benchmarks
  • 4 Contributed to improved rim durability and manufacturing processes

Technical Implementation

The impact testing system was designed to replicate the forces and impacts that carbon rims experience during normal and extreme riding conditions. This involved creating controlled impact scenarios and measuring rim response under various load conditions.

Data collection included force measurements, deformation analysis, and failure mode documentation, providing comprehensive insights into rim performance and durability characteristics.

Impact and Results

The testing protocols I developed became part of Boyd Cycling's standard quality control process. The insights gained from this project contributed to design improvements and manufacturing refinements that enhanced the overall durability and performance of their carbon rim products.
