Project 1

RTM Process Optimization

Enhancing Carbon-Rim Fabrication Quality & Consistency

The Challenge

Boyd Cycling's RTM (Resin Transfer Molding) process was experiencing inconsistent resin distribution, leading to dry zones in carbon rims and variable product quality. Manufacturing efficiency was compromised by unpredictable fabrication outcomes.

Solution Approach

Conducted comprehensive analysis of mold geometry and machine calibration parameters. Overhauled the entire RTM process by redesigning mold flow patterns and optimizing injection parameters to achieve uniform resin distribution and eliminate dry zones.

Impact

Standardized resin distribution across all carbon rim production, improving manufacturing consistency and product quality. Enhanced production efficiency and reduced material waste through optimized process parameters.

Key Achievements

Eliminated dry zones in carbon rim fabrication
Standardized resin distribution process
Improved manufacturing consistency
Enhanced product quality standards
Project 2

Custom Impact Testing Development

UCI-Compliant Wheel Durability Validation

The Challenge

Boyd Cycling needed reliable, consistent impact testing capabilities to validate carbon rim durability according to UCI standards. Existing commercial testing equipment was cost-prohibitive and lacked customization for specific rim geometries.

Solution Approach

Conceived and built a custom wheel-impact rig utilizing winch and electromagnetic stopper systems. Designed the testing apparatus to deliver precise, repeatable impacts while meeting UCI standard requirements for comprehensive quality assurance.

Technical Implementation

Integrated electromagnetic release mechanism for consistent impact delivery, developed precision control systems for repeatable testing, and implemented safety protocols for reliable operation in production environment.

Technical Specifications

Release System Electromagnetic
Control Method Winch + Stopper
Testing Standard UCI Compliant
Application Rim Durability
Project 3

International Technology Transfer

European Composite Protocols to U.S. Operations

The Challenge

Time Bicycles needed to establish U.S. manufacturing capabilities at CU-ICAR while maintaining the quality and precision of their European composite injection processes. This required adapting established protocols to new facilities and equipment.

Solution Approach

Transferred European carbon-injection protocols to CU-ICAR facilities, adapting established manufacturing processes to American operations. Collaborated across international teams to ensure process fidelity and quality standards.

Cross-Cultural Impact

Successfully accelerated Time Bicycles' U.S.A. rollout by implementing proven European manufacturing techniques. Demonstrated technical adaptability and cross-cultural collaboration skills in high-stakes manufacturing environment.

Key Contributions
